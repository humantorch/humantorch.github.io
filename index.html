Hi, I’m Scott. I’ve spent 25 years in tech, and about a decade ago I accidentally became a manager, following the all-too-common career path of “hey, this guy writes really good code, we should put him in charge of people.” I was wholly unprepared for it. I’ve been learning and making mistakes as a leader ever since, and I wouldn’t trade it for anything.

I started as a graphic designer in 2001, right as the dot-com bubble burst, because I’ve never been much for conventional wisdom. Quickly learned I was bad at design and good at code, and followed that thread until someone handed me a team and wished me luck. These days my work sits at the intersection of engineering, product, and go-to-market, turning ambiguous strategy into execution and building systems and teams that hold up when I’m not in the room.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

What I Do
